## What it is

A Static Max Drawdown is the maximum amount your account can fall from the starting balance before the account is hard-breached. Unlike the trailing version, a static drawdown is set at a **fixed percentage of the starting balance** and **does not move** as the account grows.

Some One-Step and Two-Step plan variants use a static drawdown instead of a trailing one. Your trader dashboard will show the exact rule that applies to your active plan.

## How it works

- At account open, the static drawdown is set at a defined % of your starting balance.
- This threshold **does not change** regardless of how high the account grows.
- If account balance/equity falls to this threshold at any point, it's a **hard breach**.

## When you'd encounter it

Static drawdown variants are typically offered on specific One-Step and Two-Step plan configurations. If your plan uses a static drawdown, that's clearly shown on your dashboard. If you're unsure which one your account uses, contact support@hybridfunding.io.

## Common questions

**Q:** Which is "better" — static or trailing drawdown?
**A:** Neither is universally better. A static drawdown gives you the same floor regardless of how the account grows, which can feel more forgiving on a hot streak. A trailing drawdown protects locked-in gains better once the account reaches the trailing-percent return and locks at starting balance. Pick based on how you trade.

**Q:** How do I know which I have?
**A:** Check your trader dashboard. If still unsure, contact support@hybridfunding.io with your account ID.
