## What it is

You are prohibited from using any trading strategy that is expressly prohibited by Hybrid Funding or the Liquidity Provider. Engaging in Prohibited Trading can result in account termination, forfeiture of fees, and refusal of a funded account.

## What's prohibited

Prohibited Trading includes (but is not limited to):

- Exploiting errors or latency in the pricing or platform(s) provided by the Liquidity Provider
- Utilizing non-public or insider information
- Front-running trades placed elsewhere
- Trading in any way that jeopardizes the relationship between Hybrid Funding and a Liquidity Provider, or that may result in the cancellation of trades
- Trading in any way that creates regulatory issues for the Liquidity Provider
- Utilizing any third-party strategy, off-the-shelf strategy, or one marketed to pass challenge accounts
- Attempting to arbitrage a funded account with another account at Hybrid Funding or a third-party firm
- Opening a position within **3 minutes before or after a News Event** — see [News Events](news-events.md)
- **Gambling practices** — taking excessive risks such as utilizing maximum leverage to open oversized positions hoping to hit profit targets in a single price movement

## Single Session Equities

For SSE, the following are also explicitly treated as Prohibited Practices and trigger a **hard breach**:

- Holding a position past **15:55 ET**
- Holding any trade for **less than 1 minute** (the minimum hold time)

## Consequences

If Hybrid Funding detects Prohibited Trading:

- Program participation may be **terminated**
- Any **fees paid** may be **forfeited**
- The trading activity will be reviewed by Hybrid Funding and the Liquidity Provider before a funded account is issued — if Prohibited Trading is found during evaluation, the trader will **not** receive a funded account

Hybrid Funding reserves the right to disallow or block any trader from participating in the program for any reason, at its sole and absolute discretion.

## Common questions

**Q:** Can I use an EA / automated strategy?
**A:** Yes, subject to the Prohibited Trading policy. See [Automated Strategies](automated-strategies.md).

**Q:** What's the "no gambling" rule in practice?
**A:** It's a discretionary anti-pattern check. Repeatedly using maximum leverage to take oversized single-trade positions in hopes of hitting a profit target in one move is the canonical example. Trade with disciplined risk management — consistent position sizing, sensible stop losses, multiple trades over multiple sessions.

**Q:** Can I copy trades from another account?
**A:** Only if **you own all the accounts** involved. Group trading, signal services, and passing services are not allowed. See [Copy Trading](copy-trading.md).
