## What it is

If you do **not place a trade at least once every 30 consecutive days** on any active account, your account is considered inactive and is **hard-breached**. The account is terminated.

## How it applies

- The rule applies to evaluation accounts and funded accounts alike.
- Each account is tracked independently. Activity on one account does not satisfy the rule on a different account.
- The 30-day counter resets every time you place a trade.

## How to avoid it

If you're traveling, on vacation, or otherwise away from your desk, place at least one trade (even a small one closed quickly) before the 30-day window expires. Plan ahead — once breached, the account cannot be reinstated for inactivity.

## Common questions

**Q:** Does opening a position and immediately closing it count as a "trade" for inactivity purposes?
**A:** Yes — any trade execution resets the 30-day inactivity counter. Subject of course to the Minimum Hold Time rule if you're on Single Session Equities (see [SSE-specific rules](sse-specific-rules.md)).

**Q:** If my account is terminated due to inactivity, can I still withdraw profits?
**A:** Inactivity is a hard breach, which means the funded account is forfeited. If there are gains in the account at the time of breach, those are paid out per the standard hard-breach-with-gains policy — see [Breach With Gains](../05-breaches/breach-with-gains.md).

**Q:** Does Single Session Equities have a separate inactivity rule?
**A:** This 30-day inactivity rule is documented for One-Step, Two-Step, and Instant Funding. SSE-specific rules are in [Single Session Equities](../02-programs/single-session-equities.md); contact support@hybridfunding.io if you need an inactivity clarification for SSE specifically.
