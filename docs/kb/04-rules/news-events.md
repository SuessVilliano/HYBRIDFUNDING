## What it is

**Opening a position within 3 minutes before or after a News Event is prohibited.**

## What happens if you violate this rule

A trader identified as having opened a position during a News Event may have:

- The offending position **closed** and the associated P&L **removed** from the account
- The **leverage on the account reduced**
- The **account breached** altogether

Hybrid Funding has sole and absolute discretion in determining what constitutes a News Event. The rule is meant to protect program integrity — it is not intended to penalize traders who inadvertently trade through an event.

## How to comply

- Maintain awareness of major scheduled economic releases and central-bank announcements relevant to the instruments you trade.
- If you have an open position approaching a known News Event, close it at least 3 minutes before the event.
- Do not open a new position within 3 minutes after a known News Event.

## Common questions

**Q:** What counts as a News Event?
**A:** Hybrid Funding has sole and absolute discretion. Generally this means high-impact scheduled economic releases (NFP, CPI, FOMC, central-bank rate decisions, major geopolitical announcements), but the list is not exhaustive.

**Q:** If I had a position open before the News Event window and held through it, is that a violation?
**A:** The rule specifically addresses **opening** a position within 3 minutes before or after a News Event. Positions held through an event without modification fall outside the explicit rule text, but the firm reserves discretion under the broader Prohibited Trading framework — be conservative and close before known events.

**Q:** Does this apply to Single Session Equities?
**A:** This rule is documented for Forex/CFD programs. SSE traders should still avoid trading immediately around scheduled events given the short session and 15:55 ET cutoff.
