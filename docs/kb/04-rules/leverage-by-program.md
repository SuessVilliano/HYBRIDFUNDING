## Leverage caps

| Asset class | One-Step | Two-Step | Instant Funding | Single Session Equities |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Forex | Up to 20:1 | Up to 30:1 | Up to 50:1 | — |
| Metals | Up to 20:1 | Up to 30:1 | Up to 50:1 | — |
| Indices | Up to 10:1 | Up to 10:1 | Up to 10:1 | — |
| Oil | Up to 5:1 | Up to 5:1 | Up to 5:1 | — |
| Cryptocurrencies | Up to 2:1 | Up to 2:1 | Up to 2:1 | — |
| Equities (S&P 100) | — | — | — | 2:1 |

## Why leverage varies

Higher leverage means a smaller margin requirement for the same notional position size — and therefore larger position sizes for the same equity. Programs with higher leverage caps require more careful risk management since the same daily move can wipe a larger portion of the account.

## Common questions

**Q:** Is the leverage listed the same as my actual position-size cap?
**A:** Leverage sets the **margin requirement** ratio. Maximum position size is generally determined by your available margin, but Hybrid Funding reserves the right to limit max order size or open positions per account at any time. See [Position Limits](../05-breaches/) discussion in the rules.

**Q:** Can I get higher than 50:1 leverage on Forex?
**A:** Not under any of our current published programs.
