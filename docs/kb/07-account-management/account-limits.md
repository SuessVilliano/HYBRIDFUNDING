## Evaluation limits (One-Step / Two-Step)

You are allowed to take **one evaluation of a specific account size and plan tier at a time across all trading platforms**.

**Example:** You can have one 100k One-Step Plan and one 100k Two-Step Plan active simultaneously. But you **cannot** have one 100k One-Step on DXtrade and another 100k One-Step on cTrader. You also cannot have two 100k One-Step plans on any combination of platforms.

### Maximum evaluation cap

- **$1 million** in active evaluation plans per person, across all combinations of size and plan tier.

## Funded account limits (One-Step / Two-Step)

- **$1 million** in active funded plans per person.

If you pass multiple evaluations and end up with two active funded accounts of the **same account size**, you have two options:

1. Keep one funded account active at a time and wait for the other to be breached before activating it, **or**
2. If no trading has taken place in either account, opt to **combine** the two accounts into a single account of double the size.

## Instant Funding limits

- **$190,000 in active Instant Funding plans per person.**
- Up to **one of each plan size**.

## Compounding (One-Step / Two-Step)

- **No limits on compounding.**
- You can start with up to $1 million of initial funding and grow the account to any balance you choose — $10 million, $20 million, and beyond.

## Common questions

**Q:** Can I have a One-Step plan and an Instant Funding plan at the same time?
**A:** Yes — they're tracked separately. The One-Step ($1M cap) and Instant Funding ($190k cap) limits are independent.

**Q:** What about Single Session Equities — does that count against these caps?
**A:** SSE is a separate program from the Forex/CFD evaluation tiers and Instant Funding. Limits for SSE are not currently bundled with the WL1/Instant caps. For specifics on multiple SSE plans, contact support@hybridfunding.io.

**Q:** I want to combine two passed funded accounts. How?
**A:** Contact support@hybridfunding.io with both account IDs. The accounts must be the **same size** and must have **no trading activity** in either of them for the combine to be possible.
