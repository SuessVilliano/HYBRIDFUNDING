## What "notional funding" means

The capital in your funded account is **notional** and may not match the amount of capital on deposit with the Broker. A funded account is notionally funded when the **actual equity** in the account (the cash backing it) differs from the **nominal account size** (the figure that establishes initial account value and your trading levels).

The **notional funds** are the difference between the nominal account size and the actual capital in the funded account.

## What this means for you in practice

- Your trading levels (position sizes, margin, leverage) are based on the **nominal** account size — e.g. a $100,000 funded account lets you trade as a $100,000 account regardless of the actual cash backing it.
- The **rules don't change** because of notional funding. Daily Loss Limit, Max Drawdown, position limits, soft/hard breaches — all apply identically as if the funding were not notional.
- Market pricing and execution are **real** and come from the Liquidity Provider. Hybrid Funding does not modify pricing, spreads, or commissions.

## Who's the counterparty to my trades?

For purposes of managing risk and minimizing transaction costs, Hybrid Funding may offset or negate market risk and act as the **direct counterparty** to certain trades initiated in the account. Such trades are executed at prices provided by the Broker.

This framework is intended to ensure traders receive real market execution while Hybrid Funding manages risk dynamically by routing existing positions or future orders to third parties as appropriate.

**Conflict-of-interest disclosure:** When Hybrid Funding acts as counterparty, the trader's gains do not net to a loss for Hybrid Funding the way they would if a third party were counterparty. The trader's calculated P&L and rule application are unchanged regardless of who the counterparty is — but the structural conflict exists and is disclosed transparently here.

## Common questions

**Q:** Does Hybrid Funding manipulate pricing or execution on my funded account?
**A:** No. Hybrid Funding does not modify pricing or execute differently. Market pricing and trade executions are provided by the Broker / Liquidity Provider. Transaction costs are not marked up — no adjusted bid-offer spreads, markups/markdowns, modified commissions, or modified swaps.

**Q:** Does notional funding mean my trades are "simulated"?
**A:** The execution is real (provided by the Broker). The capital backing the account is what's notional — i.e., the firm provides the trading capital rather than the trader depositing the full nominal amount.
