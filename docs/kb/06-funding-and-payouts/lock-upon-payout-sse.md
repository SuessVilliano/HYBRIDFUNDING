## What it is

**Lock Upon Payout** is a setting that is **enabled by default** on all Single Session Equities plans. When enabled:

- Once a payout is processed, the **Max Drawdown is permanently locked at the original starting balance** of the funded account.
- From that point forward, the account balance may not fall below the starting balance. If it does, the account is hard-breached.

## Example

- Funded account starting balance: **$100,000**
- Account grows to: **$110,000**
- Trader requests and receives a payout
- Max Drawdown locks at **$100,000** (the starting balance)
- The account can keep trading, but if the balance falls back to $100,000, the account is breached

## Disabling Lock Upon Payout

There's an optional add-on available **at checkout** — **+25% of plan price** — that disables Lock Upon Payout for that plan. With the add-on:

- The Max Drawdown continues to operate as a trailing drawdown after payouts (rather than locking at starting balance).
- This gives the trader more room to keep compounding the account after taking payouts.

## When this add-on makes sense

- If you plan to take periodic payouts while continuing to grow the account, disabling Lock Upon Payout gives you a more forgiving drawdown floor.
- If you're a "harvest and reset" trader (planning to withdraw most gains and let the account run smaller), the default Lock Upon Payout is fine.

## Common questions

**Q:** Does the add-on cost get refunded if I never use it?
**A:** No. The add-on is a one-time fee at checkout that applies for the life of the plan.

**Q:** Can I add this on after purchase?
**A:** No. The add-on must be selected at checkout.

**Q:** Does Lock Upon Payout exist on Forex/CFD programs?
**A:** The equivalent mechanic on One-Step, Two-Step, and Instant Funding is that the Max Trailing Drawdown **locks at the starting balance on withdrawal** — see [Withdrawal and Trailing Drawdown](withdrawal-and-trailing-drawdown.md). It's the same idea under a different name; the SSE-specific Lock Upon Payout add-on lets you opt out, which is unique to SSE.
