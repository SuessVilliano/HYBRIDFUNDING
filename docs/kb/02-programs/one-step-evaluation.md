## What it is

The One-Step Evaluation is a single-phase challenge. You demonstrate consistent, disciplined trading by hitting the profit target on a single account without violating the Daily Loss Limit, Max Drawdown, or any other rule. Pass once, then you receive a funded account.

## Leverage caps

One-Step plans offer up to:

- **20:1 leverage** on Forex and Metals
- **10:1 leverage** on Indices
- **5:1 leverage** on Oil
- **2:1 leverage** on Cryptocurrencies

## Rules that apply

The One-Step Evaluation follows the same core rules that apply to the Funded Account:

- Daily Loss Limit — see [Daily Loss Limit](../04-rules/daily-loss-limit.md)
- Max Trailing Drawdown — see [Max Drawdown (Trailing)](../04-rules/max-drawdown-trailing.md)
- 30-day inactivity rule — see [Inactivity Rule](../04-rules/inactivity-rule.md)
- Weekend close-out — see [Weekend Holding](../04-rules/weekend-holding.md)
- News event prohibition — see [News Events](../04-rules/news-events.md)
- No prohibited trading strategies — see [Prohibited Trading](../04-rules/prohibited-trading.md)

The specific profit target percentage and trailing drawdown percentage depend on the account size and plan you purchase. Both are visible on your trader dashboard for any active plan.

## After you pass

You'll receive an email with instructions to complete your KYC verification and Trader Agreement. Once both are complete, your funded account is typically created and issued within 24–48 business hours. See [Funded Account Delivery](../06-funding-and-payouts/funded-account-delivery.md).

## Common questions

**Q:** Is there a time limit to pass the One-Step Evaluation?
**A:** Beyond the 30-day inactivity rule (you must place a trade at least once every 30 days), there is no maximum time limit to complete the evaluation.

**Q:** What happens if I fail the evaluation?
**A:** If you breach a hard rule (Daily Loss Limit, Max Drawdown, or 30-day inactivity), the evaluation account is closed. You can purchase a new evaluation at any time.

**Q:** Can I have multiple One-Step plans at once?
**A:** See [Account Limits](../07-account-management/account-limits.md). You can have one One-Step plan of a given account size active across all platforms.
