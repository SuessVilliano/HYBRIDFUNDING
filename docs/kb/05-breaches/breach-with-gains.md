## What happens to my gains if my funded account hard-breaches?

If you have **gains** in your funded account at the time of a hard breach, you **still receive your portion** of those gains based on the profit split.

## Example

- Funded account starting balance: **$100,000**
- Account grows to: **$110,000** (closed balance)
- A hard breach occurs at $110,000

Of the **$10,000 in gains**, the trader receives their share per the profit split (e.g. 80% under the standard 80/20 split = $8,000 to trader, $2,000 retained by the firm).

## How to claim those gains

After the breach, gains owed are processed through the standard withdrawal flow. KYC must be passed first (if not already passed). Withdrawal payment methods are the available outbound payment options at that time.

## What about losses past the starting balance?

If the breach occurred because the account fell below the starting balance (e.g., trailing drawdown locked at $100,000 and equity fell to $100,000), there are no gains to distribute — the account closes with no profit payout.

## Common questions

**Q:** Does this apply to Single Session Equities?
**A:** SSE has the Payout Protector add-on specifically to protect eligible profit share in the event of a hard breach. Without the add-on, the profit-share treatment around a breach in SSE may differ. See [Payout Protector](../08-add-ons/payout-protector.md). For an authoritative answer in a specific SSE breach scenario, contact support@hybridfunding.io.

**Q:** Do I still need to pass KYC if I'm only claiming breach-related gains?
**A:** Yes. KYC is required before any payout is processed, regardless of whether the account is still active.
