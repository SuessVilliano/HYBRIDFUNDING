## What it is

There are two categories of rule violations: **soft breaches** and **hard breaches**. They have very different consequences.

## Soft Breach

A **soft breach** means the trades that violated the rule are closed, but the account itself is not terminated. You can continue trading.

The canonical soft breach is **weekend holding without the Weekend Hold add-on**: positions left open past 3:45 PM EST Friday are auto-closed, but the account continues.

## Hard Breach

A **hard breach** means the account is **terminated** (funded account forfeited; evaluation account closed).

Hard breaches include:

- Violating the **Daily Loss Limit**
- Violating the **Max Drawdown** (static or trailing)
- Violating the **30-day inactivity** rule (Forex/CFD programs)
- For Single Session Equities:
  - Holding a position past **15:55 ET**
  - Other Prohibited Practices specific to SSE
- Engaging in **Prohibited Trading** (news-event violations, off-the-shelf or third-party strategies, gambling-pattern trading, etc.)
- EA / automated-strategy violations

## What happens to gains at the time of a hard breach

If you had **gains** in your funded account at the time of a hard breach, your portion of those gains is still paid out. See [Breach With Gains](breach-with-gains.md).

## Can a hard breach be reversed or appealed?

Hard breaches are generally final. In rare cases of demonstrable technical malfunction (platform outage, market-data error attributable to the system), contact support@hybridfunding.io with timestamps and any platform error messages. There is no general appeals process for trader-initiated breaches.

## Common questions

**Q:** Are pending orders closed at the time of a breach?
**A:** Yes — when an account is breached, open positions are closed and pending orders are cancelled.

**Q:** Does a breach on one of my accounts affect my other accounts?
**A:** Each account is treated independently for breach purposes. However, repeated breaches or patterns suggesting Prohibited Trading across accounts may trigger a broader review under Hybrid Funding's discretionary authority.

**Q:** I think my account was breached by mistake. What do I do?
**A:** Contact support@hybridfunding.io with your account ID, the time of the suspected breach, and any screenshots of platform behavior at that time.
