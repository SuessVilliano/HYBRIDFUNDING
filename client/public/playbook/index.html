04 · Trailing Max Drawdown

How the Trailing Floor Works

The trailing drawdown follows your closed balance upward — it never moves down. Once your closed balance locks it at your starting balance, you're protected forever. The worked example below uses a $100K Forex 1-Step account with a 6% trailing drawdown.

Day What Happened Closed Balance DD Floor (6% below peak)
Start — $100,000 $94,000
Day 1 +$2K winner closed $102,000 $96,000 (trailed up)
Day 2 −$1.5K loser closed $100,500 $96,000 (no change — floor never drops)
Day 3 +$5.5K winner closed $106,000 LOCKED at $100,000 forever
Day 4+ Continue trading Whatever $100,000 — can never breach original capital
The #1 funded trader strategy: Ladder to your lock threshold first. Most blow-ups happen between Day 1 and the lock. Get to that threshold before sizing up or chasing the profit target.

10 · How Traders Breach (And How Not To)

The 5 Most Common Blow-Ups

Most breaches are preventable. They fall into the same five patterns. Study these and you've already eliminated 80% of your risk.

1
The Friday Trap
Trader leaves a Forex position open over the weekend without the Weekend Hold add-on. The platform auto-closes at 3:45 PM EST Friday — potentially mid-move at a loss.
Set a phone alarm for 3:30 PM Friday. Or buy the Weekend Hold add-on if you swing trade.
2
The "Hold It Longer" Trap
Trader runs a small drawdown, adds to a losing position to "hold through it," and trails their DD floor down to a breach in one session. Common on 1-Step and IF Lite with tight trailing DDs.
Never add to a losing position on a trailing-DD account. Take the small loss, reset, and re-enter with conviction.
3
The Consistency Breach
Funded Futures trader has a monster day — 35% of all profit made in one session. Violates the 25% consistency cap. Account is flagged even though they're profitable.
If you're having a big day, scale down size mid-session once you approach 20% of total profit. Spread your edge across days.
4
The Intraday Equity Breach (SS Equities)
SS Equities has a 2.5% intraday trailing DD on open equity — not just closed. A trade that's open and down 2.5% from the day's high equity triggers the breach, even if no position is closed.
Treat your intraday trailing floor like a hard stop. Monitor open equity, not just closed P&L.
5
The Inactivity Breach
Some programs require minimum trading activity. Trader goes 30+ days without placing a trade. Account is flagged or closed for inactivity.
Even if you have nothing to trade, place one small qualifying trade per month to keep the account active. Check your specific program's inactivity policy.
Three rules that prevent most breaches: Keep position size small. Lock the trailing floor before laddering up. Close winners when you have them — don't let green go red.
